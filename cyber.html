Security Online

Index Page

In today's digital age, cybersecurity is more important than ever. Protecting your personal information and online activities from cyber threats is crucial.

  1. Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts.
  2. Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  3. Keep your software and operating systems up to date.
  4. Be cautious of phishing scams and suspicious links.
  5. Regularly back up important data.

USB Rubber Ducky looks like a thumb drive but behaves like a tiny, ruthless typist — it injects keystrokes at superhuman speed to automate tasks or simulate how a bad actor might exploit unattended machines. Security pros use it for red-team testing and to teach organisations how a single plugged-in device can bypass naive physical security; ethically it’s a tool for exposure and remediation, not abuse.

The WiFi Pineapple is a powerful wireless auditing rig: it maps, impersonates, and analyses Wi-Fi networks to reveal weak spots in how devices and people connect. Pentesters use it to stress-test enterprise and consumer Wi-Fi setups — again, its value is in uncovering weaknesses to fix them, not exploiting them; treat it like a scalpel, not a club.
